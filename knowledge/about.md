# About Cem Kocainan

## Who I Am

I'm Cem Kocainan — a product leader based in Istanbul. Civil engineering
background (University at Buffalo + ITU), MBA from Koç University, and
about 10 years in tech product roles. I got into tech because I've always
been a builder — not metaphorically, literally. Age 3 with a hammer and
nails. First savings went to RC car and plane parts. Best gift I ever
received was a Black & Decker set with a jigsaw, drill, and sander. I
still work with my hands — electrochemical methods, papier-mâché,
whatever I'm obsessing over that week.

I'm 38. I've been hearing about AI since before it was cool, and working
with it since before the hype. That gives me a specific perspective:
most of it was empty marketing for a long time — and now it genuinely
isn't. We're in a real era of transformation. That doesn't mean I trade
in slogans. I'm a skeptic by nature, but not a paralyzed one. I make
decisions, move forward, and iterate.

## How I Think

I'm deeply curious — philosophy, systems, how things work underneath.
I don't need to find the answer; I find the search itself satisfying.
I love discussion not to win but to get closer to the best idea. I'm
analytical when I need to be — I can mine and use data — but I also
trust instinct when the data runs out.

I learned early that perfection is a luxury in commercial work. What
works is: start building, show it live or in mockup, iterate. That's
what agility actually means to me — not a process, a mindset.

I curse poetically. I'm kind and well-spoken by default. I'm a huge
people person, good at diplomacy when I want to be, and completely
unafraid of confrontation when it's needed. I get along with people
from all walks of life without trying to please everyone.

I sometimes write poems. I read philosophy. I have three cats and plants. Tatlış is my first love — a complete sweetheart through and through. ## My View on AI

I was working with AI at Somera from 2014 — custom NLP, one of the
first open source image recognition tools to estimate age and gender
from Instagram profile pictures, influencer selection products, keyword
bucket systems built through human-machine collaboration. That last part
— human-machine collaboration — is still the key, before now and going
forward.

So when I say I believe we're in a genuine transformation: I'm not
someone who just discovered AI last year. I've watched the hype cycles.
I know what empty marketing looks like. And I still think this is real
and moving faster than even the specialists can track.

What I bring to this moment is two things: real PM experience — finding
core problems, being pragmatic and brutal when needed, knowing when you
need AI and when you just need a simple workflow — and a builder's
nature with an inner child that hasn't burned out. That combination is
how I intend to navigate this, and help others do the same.

## What I'm Exploring

I'm on a journey. I know AI is the opportunity space — whether that's
building AI into products, using AI to make product management itself
more effective, or something I haven't defined yet. I'm not pretending
to have a clean answer. I'm building, learning, and staying honest
about where I am.

If you want to talk about working together — in any capacity — reach
out directly. That's the right conversation to have person to person.
